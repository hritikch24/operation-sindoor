Operation Sindoor: India's Strategic Response to Pahalgam Terror Attack

India carried out a series of precision airstrikes on nine identified terror hubs across Pakistan and Pakistan-Occupied Kashmir (PoK) in retaliation for the April 22 Pahalgam attack, which killed 25 Indian civilians and one Nepali citizen. This marked the first time since the 2019 Balakot strikes that Indian warplanes crossed the Line of Control (LoC).

The strikes, conducted under the codename Operation Sindoor, were carried out by elite IAF Mirage 2000 fighter jets armed with Israeli-made SPICE 2000 precision-guided munitions. The operation was supported by Sukhoi Su-30MKI aircraft that provided air cover during the mission. These fighter jets, known as "Vajra" (thunderbolt), have a proven track record from the 1999 Kargil War and the 2019 Balakot airstrikes.

Prime Minister Modi had earlier vowed to pursue the attackers "to the ends of the earth" following the Pahalgam massacre. The Indian military was given "complete operational freedom" to respond to the attack, resulting in today's decisive action against terrorist infrastructure used to plan and direct cross-border operations.
